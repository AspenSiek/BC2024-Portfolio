My Process for My Portfolio Project

Brainstorm

Before doing anything else, I did some rough sketches of what I wanted each of my pages to look like.

Wireframe

Next, I went onto Whimsical to do some more precise modeling for my website. Not all of the features I included ended up in my finished product.

HTML

The NavBar took a few tries and W3 School searches to get right, but I ended up with this list or links with divs inside of it. I also practiced making columns, rows, and lines in HTML while making my skills page.

CSS

I also learned how to make translucent white backgrounds during this project (using the RGBA value rbga(255, 255, 255, 0.85)) which helped me create the style I was looking for.

The achievement I am most proud of is figuring out animations and transitions, two of which are shown here.

The animation takes care of sliding all of the content onto the page when it loads. I did that by changing the content's left margin from the entire page's width to zero.

The transition increases the width of project enclosures from 90% to 93% when the user hovers over the div.
